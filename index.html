Make podcasts searcheable!

Speech Recognition for OACHKATZLSCHWOAF


One of the biggest challenges for podcasters to attract audience from search engines is to provide enough/the right keywords. The best way would be to have entire transcripts to make the content of podcasts/videocasts searchable. Speech recognition has made remarkable progress, but is still focused on standard language and doesn’t really cover Austrian Standard German. We have access to an archive of more than 100.000 audios, we want to build a pipeline to get to transcripts automatically. To make the annotation of transcript easier, we want to build a pipeline for our podcast community. With the derived data we want to improve our model. The transcript we put into the metadata and make the podcast searchable. Since speech recognition is still a major challenge, we hope to be able to start by working with transcripts that only partially cover the content, but can provide enough information to perform improved automated tagging.

A solution for the European language challenge in the media market

How we do it

1. Data sources

Potential sources are videostories, podcasts and radio shows in Austrian Standard German (or other languages later on).

2. Speech recognition

With speech recognition we (i) provide metadata to make audios/videos searchable; (ii) have the data basis for further NLP analysis.

3. Classification

Based on the data provided in step 2 classification can be run.

4. Recommendation & Syndication

With the classification results and specific userdata (if available), recommendations can be offered. By syndicating platforms with similar content, network effects can be gained and additional audience attracted. By adding automated translations, the potential audience can be Europe-wide.

What we’ve done at the ready2order hackathon

We did research on open source language models for standard German as a basis for training our own algorithm. KALDI appeared as the current standard for open source language recognition training. Language models are available, for example Hamburg University offers a German language model. After installed KALDI we successully processed audio data.

About US

The Cultural Broadcasting Archive is an online audio database available at https://cba.fro.at. It was launched by Radio FRO in 2000 as an exchange platform and has transformed into the biggest podcasting hosting platform in Austria. Currently there are over 100.000 audio files in 40 different languages, mostly attributed with Creative Commons licences. We are preparing a relaunch which is planned for Q1 2020.
